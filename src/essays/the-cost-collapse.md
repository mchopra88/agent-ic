This is the actual math. Not "97% margins" as a headline — the cost structure over time and the specific decisions that collapsed it.

Year one was human-intensive. Lead qualification: human agents. Inventory matching: human agents pulling from spreadsheets. Follow-up: human agents texting from personal phones. Document collection: human agents emailing back and forth. Application processing: human agents filling out forms. The cost per conversion was dominated by labor, and the labor produced inconsistent results.

The offshore pivot cut costs by 60% but introduced quality problems. US-based agents converted tours to applications at 53%. Offshore agents at 25%. The math didn't work — cheaper agents producing half the conversion rate costs you more per closed deal, not less. We cut two BPO providers after they generated zero net revenue.

The AI phase collapsed the cost structure entirely. Here's what the numbers actually look like now:

The AI subscriptions — Claude API, Gemini API, Twilio messaging, Bland AI for voice — run roughly $400-600/month at current volume. That handles lead qualification, inventory matching, follow-up cadences, document reminders, compliance checking, and the overnight machine.

The human cost that remains: a few people handling phone calls and apartment tours. That's it.

The contribution margin calculation: revenue per conversion minus (AI cost per conversion + human cost per conversion). When the AI cost per conversation is measured in fractions of a cent and the human cost is concentrated only in the final physical steps, the margin math produces numbers that shouldn't be possible in a brokerage business.

97% contribution margin is not a marketing claim. It's an accounting artifact of what happens when you remove humans from every step that doesn't require physical presence.
